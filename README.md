# CS-250---Software-Development-Cycle
## How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?
I interpret user needs by gathering requirements and understanding the goals of the customer and end users. User stories help translate those needs into clear, actionable development tasks. By focusing on what the user wants to accomplish and why it is important, user stories help ensure that the software delivers value and meets customer expectations. Throughout this course, user stories provided a roadmap that guided development and helped prioritize features based on business needs.
## How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
I approach developing programs by first understanding requirements, planning the solution, and then building functionality in small, manageable increments. Agile development encourages continuous improvement through iterative development, frequent testing, and regular feedback. In future projects, I hope to continue using sprint planning, daily communication, sprint reviews, and retrospectives because these practices improve collaboration, adaptability, and overall project quality.
## What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
Being a good team member means communicating effectively, collaborating with others, and contributing toward shared project goals. A strong team member is reliable, open to feedback, willing to help others, and committed to continuous improvement. Agile development emphasizes teamwork and transparency, making it important for every member to actively participate in planning, development, testing, and retrospectives. Successful software projects depend not only on technical skills but also on the ability to work effectively as part of a team.
